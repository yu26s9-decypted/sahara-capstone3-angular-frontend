Privacy Notice

Last updated: June 20, 2026

This Privacy Notice explains how Sahara handles information in this capstone marketplace. We aim to collect only what helps the app work, not your life story, your snack preferences, or why you opened twelve product tabs and bought nothing.

Information We Collect

Sahara may collect account details such as your username, login credentials, session token, and product interactions needed to make the app function. If you browse products, sign in, or use checkout-related features, the app may use that information to keep the experience moving.

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal information for this project. The capstone has enough responsibilities already.

How We Use Information

We use information to sign you in, keep your session active, display account-aware navigation, support shopping features, and connect the frontend to the backend API.

We may also use basic app behavior to improve the project. Translation: if something breaks, we would like enough clues to find it without staring into the console like it owes us money.

Tokens and Local Storage

Sahara may store a session token in your browser's local storage after login. This helps the app remember that you are signed in. Please do not treat local storage like a bank vault. It is useful, but it does not wear armor.

You can remove stored session data by signing out or clearing your browser storage.

Sharing Information

Sahara does not sell your personal information. This is a capstone project, not a data bazaar. Information may be sent to the backend service only when needed for app features like authentication or product actions.

Cookies

Sahara may not currently rely on cookies for core behavior, but future versions could use cookies or similar browser storage. If cookies appear later, we promise they will be browser cookies, not the chocolate chip kind. Legal pages are sadly not snack-enabled.

Your Choices

You can choose not to create an account, avoid signing in, clear local storage, or stop using the app. You can also inspect the code, because this project is learning-friendly and the frontend has nowhere to hide.

Security

We use reasonable project-level practices to protect account and session information. Still, this is an educational application, so please do not use passwords you care about. Give your real bank password the peaceful life it deserves.

Changes to This Notice

We may update this Privacy Notice as Sahara grows. If the wording changes, it probably means the developer added a feature, fixed a bug, or discovered that deployment has a personality and a flair for suspense.
